2. Extract the archive and place the raw file named exactly `malicious_phish.csv` into your project root directory (`PhishGuard_Ghana`).




⚖️ Legal & Forensic Compliance (Ghana)

The cryptographic evidence chain generated by `forensic_hashchain.py` compiles metadata into logs designed to meet regulatory standards for admissibility in Ghanaian courts as electronic documentary evidence:

 Electronic Transactions Act, 2008 (Act 772), Ss. 7–11: Authorizes the legal recognition and protection of electronic records and data messages.


Evidence Act, 1975 (NRCD 323), Section 135:** Sets conditions for the admissibility of electronic and documentary records.


Cybersecurity Act, 2020 (Act 1038), Section 4(c):** Establishes the underlying legal mandate regarding phishing offense enforcement.



  Core Generated Forensic Artifacts

Running the full pipeline produces three major compliance and verification files:

1. phishguard_evidence_chain.jsonl (Full Ledger): Complete immutable metadata log containing sequential blocks, timestamps, model confidences, and cryptographic pointer hashes.


2. phishguard_hash_registry.csv (Tabular Registry): Clean, structural table containing record numbers, timestamps, verdicts, and valid hashes—ready for submission or appendix inclusion.


3. phishguard_chain_verification.txt(Integrity Report): Automated report checking the cryptographic integrity of the entire chain to confirm it is free from tampering.
